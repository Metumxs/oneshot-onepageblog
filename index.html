OneShot is a 2D top-down puzzle-adventure game about a child named Niko, who wakes up in a desolate world with one mission ‚Äî restore its dying sun. But there's a twist.

You're not Niko.

You're you.

And the game knows that.

Niko's wide glowing eyes, small stature, and cat-like ears give them an otherworldly charm ‚Äî a child who looks like a cat, but with a softness that feels deeply human.

What starts as a quiet indie adventure quickly evolves into a metafictional experience ‚Äî one that reaches beyond the game window and into your actual desktop. Files change. Windows close by themselves. The game addresses you by name. It breaks the fourth wall, not for shock, but for emotion.

‚≠ê Core Features That Make It Special

  • Meta gameplay: OneShot alters and reads real files on your PC, sometimes asking you to find clues in your system folders.
  • You are part of the story: The game consistently separates the player (you) and the protagonist (Niko), building a dual relationship dynamic rarely explored in games.
  • Permanent consequences: The title isn't just a name ‚Äî your first playthrough is your only one‚Ä¶ unless you find a certain hidden path.
  • Emotive soundtrack: Soft piano themes, ambient layers, and lo-fi synths create an atmosphere of both warmth and decay.
  • Distinct pixel-art: The visuals feel like fragments of a dream ‚Äî washed out, minimalistic, and incredibly expressive.

You will explore a surreal world filled with the remnants of a civilization in decline. You'll interact with strange machines, lonely characters, and fragile environments that quietly tell stories.
